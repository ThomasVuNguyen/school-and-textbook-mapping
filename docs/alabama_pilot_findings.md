# Alabama Access Code Adoption — Pilot Research Findings

**Date:** March 9, 2026  
**Scope:** 55 degree-granting institutions in Alabama (23 community colleges, 32 four-year)  
**Method:** Web search + URL content analysis per school

---

## Executive Summary

**Access code adoption in Alabama is near-universal among degree-granting institutions.** The dominant model is "inclusive access" / "first day" programs where digital materials (including access codes for Pearson, McGraw-Hill, Cengage, Wiley) are bundled into per-credit-hour fees and auto-billed to students.

- **100% of public 4-year universities** have formal inclusive access programs
- **Majority of community colleges** use Barnes & Noble "First Day Complete" or similar programs
- **Private institutions** vary — larger ones have formal programs; smaller/religious ones use per-course access codes
- **OER adoption is emerging** but not yet replacing access codes at scale (UNA is a notable exception)

## Key Patterns & Observations

1. **Barnes & Noble College dominates** as the bookstore/IA delivery partner across Alabama (public 4-year + community colleges)
2. **Follett** is the alternative for a few schools (Alabama A&M, UNA, Montevallo)
3. **eCampus** is emerging (Athens State)
4. **Per-credit-hour pricing** ranges from **$21–$26.95/cr hr** for bundled programs; Alabama A&M's flat $380 is an outlier
5. **Opt-out rates** are a key metric not captured here — would require institutional data
6. **OER is not yet a significant counter-trend** in Alabama; UNA is the most visible proponent

## Methodology Assessment

### What worked well
- **Web search** was highly effective for discovering formal inclusive access programs — schools publicly announce these
- Program names, pricing, and publisher partners were consistently findable
- State-system-level research (ACCS) efficiently covered multiple schools

### What was harder
- **Smaller private institutions** had minimal online presence for textbook policies
- **Per-course access code usage** (vs. institutional programs) is not well-documented publicly
- **Opt-out rates and actual adoption numbers** are not publicly available

### Recommendations for scaling to 50 states
1. **Prioritize state systems** — many states have system-wide contracts (SUNY, Cal State, etc.)
2. **Barnes & Noble College and Follett** are the two dominant bookstore partners nationally — their program pages are good starting points
3. **Build a Python script** that automates search queries per school and parses results
4. **Expected coverage**: ~60-70% of schools can be researched automatically; ~30% will need manual review
